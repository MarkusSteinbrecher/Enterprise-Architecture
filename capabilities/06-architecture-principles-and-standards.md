# Capability 6: Architecture Principles & Standards

**Domain:** Architecture Development - Common

## Purpose

To define, maintain, and govern the guiding principles and standards that constrain and direct all architecture work, ensuring consistency, quality, and alignment across the enterprise.

## Scope

- Architecture principles definition and lifecycle management
- Technology and architecture standards definition
- Standards governance (approval, exceptions, retirement)
- Principles and standards communication and adoption

## Key Activities

- Define architecture principles (high-level, enduring guardrails) organized by domain
- Define technology standards (specific, prescriptive mandates - e.g., approved technologies, protocols)
- Establish a standards lifecycle (proposed → approved → active → retiring → retired)
- Manage the Technology Radar (adopt, trial, assess, hold)
- Govern exceptions and waivers to standards
- Communicate principles and standards to all stakeholders
- Review and update principles and standards periodically
- Align principles and standards with industry best practices and regulatory requirements
- Measure standards adoption and compliance

## Inputs

- Enterprise strategy and goals
- Industry standards and best practices (e.g., ISO, NIST, IEEE)
- Regulatory and compliance requirements
- Technology landscape and vendor roadmaps
- Architecture review feedback and lessons learned
- Innovation and emerging technology assessments (Cap 25)

## Outputs / Deliverables

- Architecture principles catalog (with rationale and implications)
- Technology standards document / Technology Radar
- Standards lifecycle register
- Exception / waiver register
- Standards compliance reports
- Standards communication materials

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Chief Architect | Approves principles and strategic standards |
| Enterprise Architect | Defines and maintains principles and standards |
| Domain Architects | Define domain-specific standards |
| Architecture Review Board | Governs standards and manages exceptions |

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | No formal principles or standards; decisions made ad-hoc |
| 2 | Repeatable | Basic technology standards defined; limited awareness and adoption |
| 3 | Defined | Principles and standards documented, communicated, and governed; Technology Radar maintained |
| 4 | Managed | Standards actively enforced; regular review cycles; exceptions formally managed; compliance measured |
| 5 | Optimizing | Community-driven standards evolution; automated compliance checking; principles embedded in design processes |

## Key Metrics / KPIs

- Number of defined principles and standards
- Standards adoption rate across projects
- Number of active exceptions / waivers
- Standards compliance rate (from architecture reviews)
- Standards review cycle frequency
- Time to approve new standards

## Dependencies

- **Target State Design** (Cap 4) - Principles and standards guide target architecture
- **Architecture Compliance & Assurance** (Cap 28) - Standards are the basis for compliance checks
- **Architecture Decision Management** (Cap 27) - Decisions governed against principles
- **Reference Architecture Library** (Cap 21) - Reference architectures embody standards
- **Innovation & Technology Radar** (Cap 25) - Emerging tech feeds standards evolution
- **Solution Design & Patterns** (Cap 12) - Standards guide solution design choices
