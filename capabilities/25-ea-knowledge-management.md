# Capability 25: EA Knowledge Management

**Domain:** Architecture Management

**Tagline:** Capture and share architecture knowledge to reduce key-person dependency.

## Definition

Establishing practices and platforms to systematically capture, organize, share and reuse architecture knowledge, decisions, patterns and lessons learned across the EA function and the wider organization.

## Objectives

Ensure critical architecture knowledge is documented and accessible – not locked in individuals' heads. Accelerate onboarding of new architects and enable consistent decision-making. Foster a culture of knowledge sharing and continuous learning within the EA community.

## Tasks

1. **Define the knowledge scope** – what needs to be captured (e.g. architecture decisions, patterns, reference architectures, lessons learned).
2. **Establish a knowledge platform or wiki** (e.g. Confluence, SharePoint) with a clear structure and taxonomy.
3. **Integrate knowledge management into daily EA work** – capture decisions and rationale as part of the architecture process.
4. **Facilitate regular knowledge sharing sessions** (e.g. architecture community of practice, brown bags).
5. **Maintain and curate knowledge base** – archive outdated content, ensure findability and relevance.

## Inputs

- Architecture decisions and decision logs
- Reference architectures, patterns and templates
- Lessons learned from projects and reviews
- EA process documentation and guidelines

## Outputs

- Structured EA knowledge base / wiki
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Reference architecture and pattern library
- Knowledge sharing session materials and recordings

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- Domain Architects (R)
- EA Analysts (C)
- All EA stakeholders (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Critical knowledge exists only in people's heads – when they leave, the knowledge is gone.
- Documentation exists but is scattered, outdated or unfindable.
- No culture of knowledge sharing – architects work in silos.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start with architecture decision records – lightweight, high-value, immediately useful.
- Make knowledge capture part of the process, not an afterthought – "no decision without a record."
- Keep it simple – a well-structured wiki beats an over-engineered knowledge management tool.
