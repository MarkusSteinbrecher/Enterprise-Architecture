# Architecture Review Board (ARB)

**Domain:** Architecture Governance, Communication & Change

**Tagline:** Provide formal governance oversight to ensure architecture decisions are consistent, compliant and of high quality.

## Definition

The Architecture Review Board (ARB) is a cross-functional governance body responsible for reviewing, challenging and approving architecture decisions across the organisation. It ensures alignment with architecture principles, standards, policies and strategic direction.

## Composition

- Chief Architect / Lead Enterprise Architect (Chair)
- Domain Architects (Business, Application, Data, Technology)
- Senior Solution / Technical Architects
- Security Architect
- Optional: CIO / CTO as executive sponsor

## Operating Model

- Regular meeting cadence (e.g. bi-weekly or monthly) with a defined agenda.
- Reviews and approves significant architecture decisions, designs and deviations.
- Provides architecture guidance and challenge to project and delivery teams.
- Manages the architecture waiver and exception process.
- Decisions are documented in a decision log and communicated to affected stakeholders and teams.
