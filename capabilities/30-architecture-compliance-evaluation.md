# Capability 30: Architecture Compliance Evaluation

**Domain:** Architecture Governance, Communication & Change

**Tagline:** Ensure architecture decisions and implementations adhere to defined standards and principles.

## Definition

Assessing and evaluating whether projects, solutions and implementations comply with established architecture principles, standards, policies and guidelines, and managing deviations through a structured waiver process.

## Objectives

Ensure that architecture standards and principles are consistently applied across projects and implementations. Identify and manage non-compliance early to reduce technical debt and architectural drift. Provide transparency on compliance status to support governance and decision-making.

## Tasks

1. **Define compliance evaluation criteria** based on architecture principles, standards and policies.
2. **Conduct architecture compliance reviews** at key project milestones (e.g. design, build, go-live).
3. **Document compliance findings,** deviations and associated risks.
4. **Manage a structured waiver process** for justified deviations – including conditions and expiry.
5. **Report compliance status** to the Architecture Review Board and relevant stakeholders.

## Inputs

- Architecture principles, standards and policies
- Project and solution architecture documentation
- Waiver requests and deviation justifications
- Previous compliance review findings

## Outputs

- Compliance assessment reports
- Deviation and waiver register
- Risk assessments for non-compliant decisions
- Compliance status dashboards and trend reports

## Roles

- Domain Architects (R)
- Architecture Review Board (A)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (C)
- Project / Solution Architects (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- No formal compliance checks – deviations are only discovered in production when it's too late.
- Compliance is perceived as a bottleneck – projects avoid or bypass architecture reviews.
- Waivers are granted informally without tracking, leading to unmanaged technical debt.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Keep reviews lightweight and integrated into existing project gates – don't add bureaucracy.
- Focus on high-risk and high-impact projects first – not everything needs a full review.
- A good waiver process is essential – saying "no" without an alternative kills credibility.
