# Capability 25: Innovation & Technology Radar

**Domain:** Architecture Management

## Purpose

To systematically scout, evaluate, and track emerging technologies, innovations, and industry trends, informing architecture strategy, standards evolution, and strategic technology adoption decisions.

## Scope

- Technology trend monitoring and scanning
- Technology radar management
- Proof of concept / proof of value execution
- Innovation pipeline management
- Vendor and startup engagement
- Emerging technology impact assessment

## Key Activities

- Monitor technology trends through analyst reports, conferences, communities, and vendor engagement
- Maintain the Technology Radar (classify technologies as Adopt, Trial, Assess, Hold)
- Evaluate emerging technologies for applicability, impact, and risk
- Conduct proofs of concept (PoCs) and proofs of value (PoVs) for promising technologies
- Manage an innovation pipeline (ideation → evaluation → PoC → adoption decision)
- Engage with technology vendors, startups, and innovation ecosystems
- Assess the impact of emerging technologies on existing architecture (AI/ML, quantum, blockchain, edge computing, etc.)
- Provide technology recommendations to architecture and business leadership
- Organize innovation events (hackathons, innovation sprints, tech showcases)
- Feed technology intelligence into standards evolution (Cap 6) and target state design (Cap 4)

## Inputs

- Industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Technology vendor roadmaps and briefings
- Startup ecosystem insights (accelerators, VCs)
- Internal innovation ideas and business challenges
- Conference and community learnings
- Business strategy and innovation goals

## Outputs / Deliverables

- Technology Radar (periodic publication)
- Technology evaluation reports
- PoC / PoV results and recommendations
- Innovation pipeline dashboard
- Technology adoption recommendations
- Emerging technology guidelines and position papers
- Innovation event outputs (hackathon results, etc.)

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Chief Architect / CTO | Sets innovation direction and priorities |
| Enterprise Architect | Leads technology radar and evaluation |
| Innovation Lead / Manager | Manages innovation pipeline and events |
| Domain Architects | Evaluate technologies in their domain |
| Solution Architects | Execute PoCs and validate technologies |

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | No systematic technology monitoring; adoption is reactive and vendor-driven |
| 2 | Repeatable | Ad-hoc technology exploration; occasional PoCs; informal sharing |
| 3 | Defined | Technology Radar maintained and published regularly; PoC framework defined; innovation pipeline active |
| 4 | Managed | Innovation pipeline systematically managed; PoC results tracked; technology intelligence feeds strategy |
| 5 | Optimizing | Innovation embedded in culture; strategic technology leadership; ecosystem partnerships driving co-innovation |

## Key Metrics / KPIs

- Number of technologies evaluated per period
- Technology Radar publication frequency
- PoC completion rate and success rate
- Technologies successfully moved to Adopt
- Innovation pipeline throughput
- Employee participation in innovation activities

## Dependencies

- **Architecture Principles & Standards** (Cap 6) - Emerging tech feeds standards evolution
- **Target State Design** (Cap 4) - Innovation influences target architecture
- **Architecture Skills & Talent Development** (Cap 24) - New technologies require new skills
- **Data Analytics & BI Architecture** (Cap 15) - AI/ML and emerging analytics capabilities
- **Reference Architecture Library** (Cap 21) - New reference architectures from proven innovations
- **Infrastructure & Cloud Architecture** (Cap 16) - New infrastructure paradigms (edge, serverless)

## Tools & Technologies

- Technology Radar tools (Thoughtworks Radar, Zalando Tech Radar, custom)
- Gartner Hype Cycle and Magic Quadrant
- Innovation management platforms (HYPE, Brightidea)
- PoC sandbox environments (cloud sandboxes, lab infrastructure)
- Vendor briefing and evaluation frameworks
- Hackathon and ideation platforms
