# Capability 28: Architecture Training

**Domain:** Architecture Governance, Communication & Change

**Tagline:** Build architecture skills and awareness across the organization to drive EA adoption.

## Definition

Planning, developing and delivering training programs that build architecture knowledge, skills and awareness among EA team members, IT staff and business stakeholders.

## Objectives

Ensure EA team members have the skills and knowledge to effectively perform their roles. Raise architecture awareness among business and IT stakeholders to foster collaboration and adoption. Establish a continuous learning culture within the EA community.

## Tasks

1. **Assess training needs** based on role requirements, skill gaps and EA maturity targets.
2. **Develop a training catalogue** covering EA frameworks, tools, methods and soft skills.
3. **Deliver training** through appropriate formats (e.g. workshops, e-learning, certifications, lunch & learns).
4. **Onboard new architects** with a structured EA onboarding program.
5. **Evaluate training effectiveness** and continuously update the training offering.

## Inputs

- EA role descriptions and competency matrix
- Skills gap analysis results
- EA tools and methodology documentation
- Stakeholder feedback and training requests

## Outputs

- EA training catalogue and learning paths
- Training materials and session recordings
- Training completion and effectiveness reports
- Onboarding program for new EA team members

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- HR / Learning & Development (C)
- Domain Architects (C)
- EA team members and stakeholders (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- No structured training – new architects learn by trial and error, leading to inconsistent quality.
- Training focuses only on tools and frameworks, neglecting communication and stakeholder skills.
- Knowledge stays with senior architects – no mechanism to transfer it to the broader team.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start with a structured onboarding program – the fastest way to improve consistency.
- Combine formal training with peer learning (e.g. community of practice, shadowing).
- Don't forget soft skills – an architect who can't communicate will struggle regardless of technical depth.
