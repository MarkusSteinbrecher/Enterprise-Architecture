# Capability 29: Communication & Change Management

**Domain:** Architecture Governance, Communication & Change

**Tagline:** Drive awareness, understanding and adoption of Enterprise Architecture across the organization.

## Definition

Planning and executing communication and change management activities to ensure that architecture initiatives, decisions and standards are understood, accepted and adopted by all relevant stakeholders.

## Objectives

Ensure architecture decisions, standards and changes are effectively communicated to all affected parties. Drive organizational adoption of EA practices by managing resistance and building buy-in. Position EA as a valued and trusted function within the organization.

## Tasks

1. **Develop an EA communication strategy and plan** covering target audiences, channels, frequency and key messages.
2. **Create and distribute regular EA communications** (e.g. newsletters, architecture updates, decision summaries).
3. **Identify change impacts** of architecture decisions and develop change management approaches.
4. **Engage stakeholders** through targeted formats (e.g. town halls, roadshows, architecture days).
5. **Monitor adoption** and gather feedback to continuously improve communication and change efforts.

## Inputs

- Architecture decisions and roadmaps
- Stakeholder map and communication preferences
- Change impact assessments
- Stakeholder feedback and adoption metrics

## Outputs

- EA communication strategy and plan
- Communication materials (newsletters, updates, presentations)
- Change impact assessments and mitigation plans
- Adoption and feedback reports

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- Communications / Change Manager (C)
- Domain Architects (C)
- CIO / CTO (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- EA works in an ivory tower – great architecture that nobody knows about or follows.
- Communication is ad-hoc and reactive – stakeholders only hear from EA when there's a problem.
- Architecture changes are pushed without change management, leading to resistance and workarounds.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Establish a regular communication rhythm – visibility builds trust and credibility.
- Tailor messages to the audience – executives need different content than developers.
- Treat every architecture change as a people change – adoption requires more than a slide deck.
