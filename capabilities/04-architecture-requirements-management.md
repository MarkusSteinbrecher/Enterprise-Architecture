# Capability 4: Architecture Requirements Management

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Common

**Tagline:** Ensure all needs are identified, understood and communicated throughout architecture development.

## Definition

Architecture Requirements Management involves managing and governing the requirements throughout the architecture development process. It ensures that all requirements are identified, documented, and addressed in the architecture.

## Objectives

Ensure all architecture requirements are systematically captured, prioritized and traceable to business goals. Manage changes to requirements in a controlled way to prevent scope creep and misalignment.

## Tasks

1. **Identify and document requirements**
2. **Analyse requirements** and prioritize based on business value, urgency and feasibility.
3. **Document requirements** in a structured form (ensure traceability to business goals) and establish and monitor baseline requirements.
4. **Manage changes to requirements** (use a repository to track changes and ensure version control).
5. **Traceability and impact analysis:** establish traceability between requirements and architecture decisions; assess the impact of changes.

## Inputs

- New stakeholder requirements.
- Business goals and objectives.
- Existing architecture documentation.

## Outputs

- Architecture requirement specifications.
- Requirement impact assessment and prioritization.
- Updated requirements repository.

## Roles

- Business Architect, Application Architect, Data Architect, Technology Architect (R)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (A)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Unclear requirements and misalignment, leading to stakeholder dissatisfaction.
- Scope creep (requirements keep getting added).
- Poor traceability (how do requirements link to the architecture and how will they be validated).

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Understand your stakeholders and clearly formulate what you want to achieve.
- Establish simple categories (must-have, nice-to-have) and push for strict prioritization; understanding why something is a must, also helps understanding requirements better.
- Standardize requirements documentation and train everyone involved on how to write requirements well.
