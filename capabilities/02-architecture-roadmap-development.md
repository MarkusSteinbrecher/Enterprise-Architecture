# Capability 2: Architecture Roadmap Development

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Common

**Tagline:** Provide a clear path towards a target architecture, aligned with business goals and priorities.

## Definition

Architecture Roadmap Development involves creating a strategic plan that outlines the steps and milestones needed to achieve a target architecture. It provides a clear timeline and prioritization from the current state to the desired future state.

## Objectives

Define a structured transition path from current to target architecture. Prioritize initiatives based on business value, strategic alignment and dependencies. Provide a clear timeline with milestones to guide execution and track progress.

## Tasks

1. **Current state assessment:** review and analyse the current state architecture to identify gaps, inefficiencies and opportunities.
2. **Target architecture definition:** reference and refine the target architecture (developed as part of the Architecture Vision).
3. **Gap analysis:** identify the gaps between the current state and the target architecture.
4. **Initiative identification:** define initiatives, projects or work packages required to address the gap.
5. **Roadmap development:** establish a timeline, including key phases and milestones.
6. **Resource planning:** identify the resource needs to execute the roadmap.

## Inputs

- Current state architecture.
- Target architecture.
- Business strategy and goals.
- Resource availabilities (budget, people, technology).

## Outputs

- Architecture Roadmap document.
- Transition architecture models.
- Implementation plans.
- Prioritized initiative backlog

## Roles

- Business Architect, Application Architect, Data Architect, Technology Architect (R)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (A)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Lack of direction and redundancies in the application landscape.
- Unrealistic and "pressured" timelines, due to a lack of strategic planning.
- Scope creep and too many scope changes.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Good understanding of the current situation and good knowledge of key dependencies.
- Well defined priorities that are based on business objectives and are broken down into incremental milestones.
- Realistic resource planning, allowing for flexibility and iteration.
- Clear, visual roadmap that is easy to understand for everyone (business and IT) and is communicated to everyone.
