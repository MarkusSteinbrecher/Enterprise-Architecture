# Capability 9: Process Modelling

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Business Architecture

**Tagline:** Visualize and optimize business processes to align operations with strategic goals and drive efficiency.

## Definition

Process Modelling involves creating visual representations of business processes to understand, analyse, and optimize how work is performed. It provides a clear view of activities, roles, systems, and information flows, enabling effective process design and improvement.

## Objectives

Create clear and comprehensive process models that enable process optimization, support business transformation initiatives, and ensure alignment between business operations and IT systems. Facilitate communication and understanding of business processes among stakeholders.

## Tasks

1. **Identify, prioritize and document key business processes** (based on business impact, complexity, transformation needs). Conduct process discovery workshops with process owners and stakeholders to understand current state processes.
2. **Analyse and document detail processes** including activities, decision points, inputs/outputs, roles and responsibilities. Create process models using standard notation (e.g., BPMN, flowcharts, swimlane diagrams).
3. **Design optimized future processes** by identifying inefficiencies, bottlenecks, redundancies, and improvement opportunities.
4. **Validate alignment** with business goals and technology. Refine process models with stakeholders and obtain approval.
5. **Maintain and update** process models as business operations evolve.

## Inputs

- Business strategy and objectives
- Stakeholder interviews and workshop results
- Existing process documentation
- System and application documentation
- Performance metrics and pain points

## Outputs

- Current state (AS-IS) process documentation
- Process improvement recommendations
- Future state (TO-BE) process models with capability and technology mapping
- Process detail documentation and descriptions

## Roles

- Business Architect (R)
- Business Analyst (C)
- Process Owner (C)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (A)
- Application Architect (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Missing, inconsistent or outdated documentation of business processes
- Lack of standardization in process modeling notation and tools
- Disconnect between business processes and IT systems, resulting in technology that doesn't support business needs.
- Limited stakeholder engagement leading to incomplete or inaccurate process models.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Use standards (tools, method, notations etc.)
- Start with high-level process models (Level 1-2) and drill down subsequently.
- Keep models simple and understandable for non-technical stakeholders.
- Focus on high-value-adding processes first, with significant business impact.
