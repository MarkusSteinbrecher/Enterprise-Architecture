# Capability 7: Business Capability Modelling

**Domain:** Business Architecture

## Description

Define and map business capabilities to understand what the business does, independent of how it is organized or what technology it uses. Business capability models provide a stable foundation for aligning strategy, technology, and organizational change.

## Objectives

- Create a comprehensive map of what the business does
- Provide a stable, organization-independent view of the business
- Enable strategic planning and investment prioritization
- Support alignment between business and IT

## Key Activities

- Business capability identification and decomposition
- Capability map creation and maintenance
- Capability heat mapping (maturity, investment, strategic importance)
- Capability-to-organization mapping
- Capability-to-application mapping
- Capability gap and overlap analysis
- Capability-based planning

## Inputs

- Business strategy and goals
- Organizational structure
- Business process documentation
- Industry reference models
- Stakeholder interviews

## Outputs / Deliverables

- Business capability map (multi-level)
- Capability heat maps
- Capability-to-organization mapping
- Capability-to-application mapping
- Capability assessment reports
- Investment prioritization inputs

## Maturity Indicators

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Initial | No formal capability model; understanding is fragmented |
| Developing | Initial capability map created; limited adoption |
| Defined | Comprehensive capability model maintained; used for planning |
| Managed | Capability model actively used for investment and alignment decisions |
| Optimizing | Dynamic capability modelling; drives continuous strategic alignment |

## Key Roles

- Business Architect
- Enterprise Architect
- Business strategists
- Domain subject matter experts

## Tools & Techniques

- Business capability mapping frameworks
- Heat mapping techniques
- Industry reference models (e.g., BIAN, APQC, eTOM)
- Architecture modelling tools
- Workshop facilitation

## KPIs / Metrics

- Capability map coverage (% of business represented)
- Capability model currency
- Number of decisions influenced by capability model
- Stakeholder adoption rate
- Capability maturity improvement over time

## Relationships

- **Value Stream Mapping** - Capabilities support value stream stages
- **Business Process Architecture** - Processes realize capabilities
- **Application Portfolio Management** - Applications support capabilities
- **Target State Design** - Capability gaps inform target state
- **Architecture Communication** - Capability maps are key communication tools
