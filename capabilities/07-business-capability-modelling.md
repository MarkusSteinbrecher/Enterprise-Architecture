# Capability 7: Business Capability Modelling

**Domain:** Business Architecture

## Purpose

To define, structure, and analyze business capabilities — what the business does — independent of organizational structure, processes, or technology, providing a stable foundation for strategic planning, investment prioritization, and business-IT alignment.

## Scope

- Business capability identification and decomposition (multi-level)
- Capability mapping and heat mapping
- Capability-to-organization and capability-to-application mapping
- Capability-based planning and investment analysis

## Key Activities

- Identify and define business capabilities at multiple levels of decomposition
- Create and maintain the enterprise business capability map
- Perform capability heat mapping (strategic importance, maturity, investment level, performance)
- Map capabilities to organizational units
- Map capabilities to supporting applications and technologies
- Identify capability gaps, overlaps, and redundancies
- Use capability model for strategic planning and investment prioritization
- Align capability model with industry reference models (e.g., BIAN, APQC, eTOM)
- Validate capability model with business stakeholders

## Inputs

- Business strategy and strategic objectives
- Organizational structure and operating model
- Industry reference capability models
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Existing business process documentation

## Outputs / Deliverables

- Business capability map (multi-level hierarchy)
- Capability heat maps (by various dimensions)
- Capability-to-organization mapping
- Capability-to-application mapping
- Capability assessment reports
- Investment prioritization inputs

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Business Architect | Leads capability modelling and maintenance |
| Enterprise Architect | Ensures alignment with enterprise architecture |
| Business stakeholders / SMEs | Validate and refine capabilities |
| Domain Architects | Link capabilities to domain architectures |

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | No formal capability model; business understanding is fragmented |
| 2 | Repeatable | Initial capability map created for a specific initiative; limited adoption |
| 3 | Defined | Comprehensive enterprise capability model maintained; used for planning and analysis |
| 4 | Managed | Capability model actively used for investment decisions and strategic alignment; heat mapping operational |
| 5 | Optimizing | Dynamic capability modelling; drives continuous strategic alignment; integrated with portfolio management |

## Key Metrics / KPIs

- Capability map coverage (% of business represented)
- Capability model currency (last updated)
- Number of strategic decisions influenced by capability model
- Stakeholder adoption rate
- Number of capability assessments completed

## Dependencies

- **Value Stream Mapping** (Cap 8) - Capabilities enable value stream stages
- **Organization & Process Modelling** (Cap 9) - Capabilities realized through processes
- **Application Portfolio Management** (Cap 10) - Applications support capabilities
- **Target State Design** (Cap 4) - Capability gaps inform target state
- **Gap Analysis & Roadmapping** (Cap 5) - Capability gaps feed roadmap
