# Capability 24: EA Process Development

**Domain:** Architecture Management

**Tagline:** Define, standardize and continuously improve the processes that drive EA delivery.

## Definition

Designing, documenting and continuously improving the core EA processes, ensuring a consistent, repeatable and efficient way of working across all architecture activities.

## Objectives

Establish standardized EA processes that ensure consistency and quality across all architecture work. Enable scalability of the EA function by reducing dependency on individual knowledge. Drive continuous process improvement based on maturity assessments and lessons learned.

## Tasks

1. **Identify and define core EA processes** (e.g. architecture development, review, governance, repository management).
2. **Document processes** including activities, roles, inputs, outputs and decision points.
3. **Align EA processes** with existing organizational frameworks (e.g. project methodology, ITIL, SAFe).
4. **Conduct periodic process maturity assessments** and identify improvement areas.
5. **Implement process improvements** and track their effectiveness.

## Inputs

- EA capability and process maturity assessment results
- Existing organizational process frameworks
- Lessons learned and feedback from EA practitioners
- Industry best practices and reference frameworks

## Outputs

- Documented EA process descriptions and workflows
- EA process maturity assessment results
- Process improvement backlog and action plans
- Updated EA methodology and ways of working

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- CIO / CTO (A)
- Domain Architects (C)
- EA Analysts (C)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- EA work is done differently by every architect – no consistent methodology or quality standard.
- Processes exist on paper but are not followed in practice.
- No mechanism to learn and improve – the same mistakes are repeated.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start by documenting the 3–5 most critical processes – don't try to formalize everything at once.
- Keep processes lightweight and practical – architects won't follow bureaucratic overhead.
- Use maturity assessments as a driver – they show where process gaps hurt the most.
