# Capability 27: Standards, Policies, Principles, Guidelines

**Domain:** Architecture Governance, Communication & Change

**Tagline:** Establish the guardrails that ensure consistency and alignment across all architecture decisions.

## Definition

Defining, maintaining and communicating the set of architecture principles, standards, policies and guidelines that govern how architecture is developed, implemented and managed across the organization.

## Objectives

Provide clear and actionable guardrails that guide architecture decisions and ensure consistency. Ensure alignment of architecture work with business strategy, regulatory requirements and industry best practices. Enable autonomous decision-making by architects within a defined framework.

## Tasks

1. **Define architecture principles** that reflect business values and strategic direction.
2. **Establish technology standards and policies** (e.g. approved platforms, security requirements, integration patterns).
3. **Develop practical guidelines** that translate principles and standards into actionable guidance for architects and project teams.
4. **Implement a review and approval process** for new or updated standards.
5. **Communicate and socialize** principles, standards and guidelines across the organization.
6. **Periodically review and update** to ensure continued relevance.

## Inputs

- Business and IT strategy
- Regulatory and compliance requirements
- Industry standards and best practices
- Architecture decision logs and lessons learned

## Outputs

- Architecture principles catalogue
- Technology standards and policies documentation
- Architecture guidelines and design patterns
- Standards review and update log

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- Architecture Review Board (A)
- Domain Architects (C)
- CIO / CTO (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- No defined standards – every project makes its own technology choices, creating fragmentation.
- Standards exist but are not communicated or enforced – they live in a document nobody reads.
- Standards are too rigid or outdated, leading architects to bypass them.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start with 5–10 core principles and a manageable set of technology standards – less is more.
- Make them visible and easy to find – embed in onboarding, project kickoffs and review processes.
- Build in a waiver process – rigid rules without exceptions kill adoption.
