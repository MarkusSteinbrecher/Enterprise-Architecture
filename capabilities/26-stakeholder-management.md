# Capability 26: Stakeholder Management

**Domain:** Architecture Management

**Tagline:** Foster stakeholder support and involvement and ensure effective communication and collaboration.

## Definition

Stakeholder Management involves identifying, analysing, and engaging stakeholders to ensure their needs and concerns are addressed throughout the architecture development process. It aims to build and maintain stakeholder support and alignment.

## Objectives

To ensure effective communication and collaboration with stakeholders, fostering their support and involvement in the architecture process. Align stakeholder expectations with architecture priorities and decisions. Build long-term trust and engagement across business and IT.

## Tasks

1. **Identify stakeholders** and create a stakeholder map. Assign priorities and identify preferred communication channels, styles and frequencies.
2. **Analyse stakeholder interests** and influence.
3. **Develop stakeholder engagement strategies.**
4. **Plan the stakeholder communication** and engagement.
5. **Regularly collect stakeholder feedback** on EA services and adjust engagement approach accordingly.
6. **Manage escalations** and resolve conflicting stakeholder interests in alignment with architecture priorities.

## Inputs

- Business strategy and goals.
- Stakeholder requirements and concerns.
- Existing stakeholder documentation.

## Outputs

- Stakeholder map.
- Stakeholder analysis reports.
- Communication and engagement plans.

## Roles

- Application Architect, Data Architect, Technology Architect (R)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (A)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Lack of trust, support and stakeholder engagement for transformation initiatives.
- Ambiguity around stakeholder roles and responsibilities, causing confusion and inefficiencies in decision-making and execution.
- Even getting a list of relevant stakeholders requires time and effort.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Identify stakeholders, document a stakeholder map and prioritize needs and expectations.
- Develop a communication plan to keep stakeholders informed and engaged.
- Tailor communication to the audience – executives need different messages than project teams.
