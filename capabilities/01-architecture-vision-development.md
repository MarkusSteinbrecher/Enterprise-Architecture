# Capability 1: Architecture Vision Development

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Common

**Tagline:** Align business strategy with architecture direction and gain stakeholder buy-in.

## Definition

Architecture Vision Development involves creating a high-level view of the desired architecture that aligns with business goals and stakeholder needs. It sets the direction for the architecture development process.

## Objectives

Align the architecture direction with business strategy and stakeholder needs. Create a shared understanding of the target architecture and the business value it enables. Secure stakeholder buy-in and signoff on the statement of architecture work.

## Tasks

1. **Stakeholder engagement:** Identify key stakeholders and their concerns (create stakeholder map; conduct interviews to gather objectives, concerns, drivers and constraints of each).
2. **Business strategy alignment:** Define the strategic goals and drivers and align the scope and constraints of the architecture effort.
3. **Develop the Architecture Vision** including all relevant artefacts (e.g. business capabilities, application landscape).
4. **Communication** to get approval / signoff.
5. **Baseline assessment** (high-level) and roadmap definition, outlining the transition to the architecture vision.

## Inputs

- Business strategy and goals
- Stakeholder concerns and requirements
- Industry trends
- Existing architecture documentation (e.g. architecture principles)

## Outputs

- Architecture Vision statement
- High-level architecture models and roadmap
- Stakeholder communication material
- Statement of architecture work

## Roles

- Enterprise Architect (R)
- CIO (A)
- Business Architect (C)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Misalignment between business and IT without a clear connection between the IT architecture effort and the business strategy.
- Lack of direction: teams are unsure what the future state looks like or why transformations are happening.
- Stakeholder resistance: key stakeholders are not engaged or do not support a transformation effort.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Understand and document the business drivers and strategic goals.
- Clearly define the scope (in-scope, out-of-scope and clear priorities).
- Use simple visuals and describe the future state (how will the business operate and how will technology enable it).
- Success metrics: define measurable outcomes.
