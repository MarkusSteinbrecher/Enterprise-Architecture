# Capability 7: Strategic Value Driver Definition

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Business Architecture

**Tagline:** Identify and articulate the key factors that drive value creation for an organization.

## Definition

Strategic Value Driver Definition is the process of identifying and articulating the key factors that drive value creation for an organization. These drivers are critical for aligning business strategies with operational activities to achieve long-term success.

## Objectives

Define clear, measurable value drivers that connect business strategy to architecture priorities. Ensure shared understanding and alignment across business and IT stakeholders.

## Tasks

1. **Stakeholder engagement:** conduct interviews and workshops to gather insights on value creation.
2. **Value driver analysis:** analyse business processes and performance data to identify potential value drivers.
3. **Documentation:** define and document strategic value drivers, including their impact on business outcomes.
4. **Strategic alignment:** align value drivers with the organization's strategic goals and objectives.
5. **Communication:** communicate the identified value drivers to stakeholders through presentations and documentation.

## Inputs

- Business strategy and objectives
- Performance data and analytics
- Stakeholder insights and expectations
- Industry benchmarks and best practices

## Outputs

- Strategic value driver documentation
- Value driver impact analysis
- Alignment matrix with business goals
- Stakeholder communication materials

## Roles

- Business Architect
- Enterprise Architect
- Business Analyst
- Stakeholders (e.g., business leaders, department heads)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Value drivers are too vague or generic, making them impossible to measure or act upon.
- Disconnect between business strategy and EA – value drivers are defined by business without architecture input, or vice versa.
- No regular review – value drivers become outdated as business priorities shift.
- Stakeholders don't understand or don't agree on what drives value, leading to conflicting priorities.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start with the top 3–5 value drivers that the business already agrees on – don't try to boil the ocean.
- Ensure each value driver is measurable – if you can't measure it, you can't prove architecture's contribution.
- Co-create with business stakeholders – value drivers defined in an EA ivory tower won't get buy-in.
- Review and update value drivers annually aligned with the strategy cycle.
