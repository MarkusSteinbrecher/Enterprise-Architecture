# Capability 22: Organisation Development

**Domain:** Architecture Management

**Tagline:** Build and evolve the EA organization to effectively deliver architecture capabilities.

## Definition

Designing, establishing and continuously improving the EA organizational structure, including team setup, roles, competencies and career paths, to ensure the EA function is adequately staffed and positioned within the organization.

## Objectives

Define and maintain an effective EA organizational structure that supports the delivery of architecture services. Ensure the right skills, competencies and capacity are available to meet current and future EA demands. Establish clear career paths and development opportunities to attract and retain EA talent.

## Tasks

1. **Define the EA organizational model** and reporting structure (e.g. centralized, federated, hybrid).
2. **Define EA roles,** job profiles and required competencies per role.
3. **Assess current team capabilities** against required competencies – identify gaps.
4. **Develop hiring plans,** training paths and career development frameworks for EA staff.
5. **Continuously evaluate and adapt** the EA organization to changing business needs and maturity levels.
6. **Foster collaboration models** between central EA and domain/solution architects across the organization.

## Inputs

- Business and IT strategy
- Current EA team structure and skill profiles
- EA capability maturity assessment results
- HR policies and frameworks

## Outputs

- EA organizational model and reporting structure
- EA role descriptions and competency matrix
- Skills gap analysis and development plans
- Collaboration model between central and federated EA roles

## Roles

- Lead Enterprise Architect (R)
- CIO / CTO (A)
- HR Business Partner (C)
- Domain Architects (I)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- EA roles are unclear or overlapping, leading to confusion about who does what.
- EA team is understaffed or lacks critical skills, but no structured plan exists to address this.
- EA is positioned too far from business or too deep in IT, limiting its effectiveness and influence.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Start with clearly defined roles and responsibilities – even a small team needs clarity.
- Align the EA org model to the company's operating model – don't copy a template blindly.
- Revisit the org setup as EA maturity grows – what works at Level 1 won't work at Level 3.
