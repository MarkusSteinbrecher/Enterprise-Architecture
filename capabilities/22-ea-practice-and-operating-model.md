# Capability 22: EA Practice & Operating Model

**Domain:** Architecture Management

## Purpose

To define and operate the Enterprise Architecture function, including its organizational structure, roles, processes, engagement model, and operating model, ensuring the EA practice delivers value effectively and is aligned with organizational needs.

## Scope

- EA operating model design and evolution
- EA team structure and roles
- EA engagement model (how EA interacts with projects, programs, and business units)
- EA process definition and optimization
- EA resource and capacity management
- EA budget and financial management

## Key Activities

- Design and evolve the EA operating model (centralized, federated, hybrid)
- Define EA team structure, roles, and responsibilities
- Define the EA engagement model (how EA engages with projects, programs, portfolios, and business units)
- Establish EA processes (architecture development cycle, review cycle, planning cycle)
- Manage EA resources, capacity, and workload allocation
- Manage EA budget and financial resources
- Onboard new architects and establish EA ways of working
- Align EA practice with Agile, SAFe, or other delivery frameworks
- Define EA service catalog (what services EA provides to the organization)
- Manage EA vendors and consulting partners

## Inputs

- Organizational strategy and structure
- EA demand and workload requirements
- EA maturity assessment results (from EA Value Management, Cap 23)
- Stakeholder feedback
- Industry best practices for EA operating models
- Budget and resource constraints

## Outputs / Deliverables

- EA operating model document
- EA team charter
- EA engagement model document
- EA service catalog
- EA process documentation
- Resource and capacity plans
- EA budget and financial reports
- EA onboarding materials

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Chief Architect | Sets EA strategy and leads the practice |
| Architecture Manager / Practice Lead | Manages day-to-day practice operations |
| Enterprise Architects | Execute EA processes and engagements |
| HR / People Manager | Supports staffing and organizational design |

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | Architecture is ad-hoc; no formal EA function or practice |
| 2 | Repeatable | EA team established; basic roles defined; EA engages reactively on projects |
| 3 | Defined | Formal EA operating model; clear engagement model; EA processes documented; service catalog defined |
| 4 | Managed | EA practice performance measured; capacity managed; continuous improvement active |
| 5 | Optimizing | EA practice as a strategic differentiator; agile and adaptive; proactively drives business value |

## Key Metrics / KPIs

- EA team utilization rate
- EA engagement coverage (% of projects/programs with EA involvement)
- Stakeholder satisfaction with EA services
- EA process cycle time
- EA headcount and capacity vs. demand
- EA practice cost / budget adherence

## Dependencies

- **EA Value Management** (Cap 23) - Value measurement validates practice effectiveness
- **Architecture Skills & Talent Development** (Cap 24) - Skills built within the practice
- **Stakeholder Management** (Cap 1) - Practice manages stakeholder relationships
- **Architecture Communication & Engagement** (Cap 26) - Practice communicates its value
- **Architecture Decision Management** (Cap 27) - Decisions made within the practice governance

## Tools & Technologies

- EA operating model templates and frameworks
- Resource and capacity management tools
- EA service management tools (ServiceNow, Jira)
- Collaboration platforms (Teams, Slack, Confluence)
- Practice performance dashboards
