# Capability 6: New Technology Evaluation

**Domain:** Architecture Development – Common

**Tagline:** Leverage new technologies to stay ahead of trends and achieve business goals.

## Definition

New Technology Evaluation involves assessing emerging technologies to determine their potential impact and value to the organization. It includes identifying, analysing, and recommending new technologies for adoption.

## Objectives

Systematically identify, assess and recommend emerging technologies based on business value, technical fit and organizational readiness. Provide a structured evaluation process to avoid ad-hoc technology decisions.

## Tasks

1. **Technology scanning:** identify emerging technologies through market research, trend analysis and vendor engagement.
2. **Feasibility assessment:** evaluate technical fit, business value, risks and organizational readiness.
3. **Proof-of-concept:** conduct pilots or prototypes to validate assumptions.
4. **Recommendation:** create technology evaluation reports with clear adoption recommendations.
5. **Standards update:** feed approved technologies into the technology standards and roadmap.

## Inputs

- Business strategy and goals.
- Technology trends and market analysis.
- Existing technology landscape.

## Outputs

- Technology evaluation reports and recommendations
- Proof-of-concept / pilot results
- Updated technology standards and approved technology list

## Roles

- Application Architect, Data Architect, Technology Architect (R)
- Lead Enterprise Architect (A)

## Common Issues with an Immature Capability

- Ad-hoc decision making has led to sub-optimal IT solutions.
- Overwhelmed teams and inability to decide on new technologies.
- High costs and low value from IT solutions.
- New technologies have been implemented but are poorly integrated.
- No formal process to evaluate new technologies.

## Pragmatic Approach – Essentials That Are Needed for Success

- Define evaluation criteria and conduct market analysis.
- Evaluate the technical- and functional-fit of new technologies.
- Conduct a proof-of-concept or pilot.
- Don't chase every trend – evaluate against concrete business needs and strategic fit.
