# Understanding of Sponsor Objectives

## What I understood

The sponsor has commissioned this ADM cycle with a clear AI transformation mandate:

| Dimension | Understanding |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Driver** | Business strategy has already defined AI ambitions — this is an architecture response to an existing strategic decision, not an exploratory exercise |
| **Focus** | AI agents specifically (not just ML/analytics) across all business capabilities |
| **Scope** | Enterprise-wide — all business units, all locations |
| **Objective 1** | Evaluate the feasibility of AI/agent adoption in business processes |
| **Objective 2** | Assess the required changes to the Enterprise Architecture across Business, Application, Data, and Technology layers |
| **Objective 3** | Define and plan the changes on an Architecture Roadmap |

## What I need clarified before proceeding

1. **Timeline and milestones** — Is there a board-level deadline for the Roadmap delivery? This determines whether we work in a 6-month sprint or an 18-month programme.
2. **Priority areas** — "All capabilities across all locations" is the full scope, but where should we start? Which business processes or capabilities are highest priority for AI adoption?
3. **Existing AI initiatives** — Are there AI/agent pilots or production systems already running? These are architectural facts we need to incorporate, not start from scratch.
4. **Success criteria** — What does "feasible" mean: technically possible, financially viable, organisationally ready, or all three?
5. **Budget envelope** — What resources (people, funding, tools) are available for this ADM cycle?
6. **Constraints** — Regulatory (EU AI Act?), data sovereignty, vendor mandates, or union agreements that constrain the architecture options?
